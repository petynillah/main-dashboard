📍Kenya National Library Service, Msanifu Kombo street. This is the oldest library in Kenya.

The first public library, Seif Bin Salim Public Library and Free Reading Area in Mombasa, was established in 1903. It was financed by Seif Bin Salim, the son of the then "Liwali" (Arab Governor) of Mombasa, Salim bin Khalfan. Though some sources cite that funds came from donations by the Asian & Indian community.

Established by Asian philanthropists to cater for the information needs of the Asian community. Asians, and in particular the Indians, were brought into Kenya in thousands to assist in the construction of the Kenya-Uganda Railway which started in Mombasa in 1898.

Seventy years later, on July 1, 1975, Seif Bin Salim Library was acquired by the Kenya National Library Service Board.
